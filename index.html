02 — Data Processing
The AI Brain

Deep learning models transform raw biological noise into ranked, actionable targets with quantified risk profiles.

1
DeepCRISPR · CRISPR-Net
In Silico Genome Screening
Deep learning models scan the entire genome at scale, mapping complex disease pathway networks. They identify "hub" genes — nodes whose modification creates a massive ripple effect across the entire disease cascade, maximizing therapeutic impact while minimizing required interventions.
GENOME SCAN HUB DETECTION PATHWAY MAPPING
2
gRNA Optimization Engine
Guide RNA Design & Risk Scoring
AI predicts optimal guide RNA (gRNA) sequences for each candidate target. It simultaneously calculates On-Target efficiency — how precisely the CRISPR complex cuts the intended location — and Off-Target risk scores, ensuring the edit won't accidentally silence critical tumor-suppressor genes or disrupt essential cellular machinery.
ON-TARGET ↑ OFF-TARGET ↓ gRNA RANKING
3
Synthetic Lethality Classifier
Pattern Recognition & Synthetic Lethality
AI identifies Synthetic Lethality pairs — gene combinations that are individually harmless, but when both are inhibited simultaneously, the cancer cell dies while healthy cells survive. This is computed across billions of possible gene pair combinations, a task impossible for human analysis alone. These pairs become ultra-precise, low-toxicity drug targets.
GENE PAIR ANALYSIS SELECTIVE KILLING CANCER-ONLY EFFECT
